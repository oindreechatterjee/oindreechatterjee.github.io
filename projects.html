AutoRemind

Started as our SIGCSE 2025 poster; now a live service built by a six-person team

An opt-in notification service that sends UC Berkeley students deadline reminders where they'll actually see them (email, SMS, or Discord) at intervals they choose. It integrates with course platforms to pull assignment deadlines and student progress in real time, acting as a single source of truth for what's due across a student's classes.

I lead the team of six developing autoremind.eecs.berkeley.edu, which is currently being piloted in Berkeley courses.

CalCoach

Team project with @anjalisujithan, @carolynwangjy, and @susanna-at

An AI scheduling coach that lives inside your Google Calendar. You tell it what you need to get done, it proposes time blocks, and you accept or reject them right inside your calendar. After each session, you give it feedback in natural language. It's not automated, it's interactive: you stay in control; CalCoach just makes the decisions easier.

Under the hood, a reinforcement learning-powered scheduling engine learns how you work best over time. A contextual bandit scores multiple schedule options in real time using signals like calendar availability, task type, timing, and user behavior. So instead of just placing tasks into open slots, CalCoach combines AI-generated schedule options with an RL-based ranking model shaped by your habits, preferences, and past feedback.
